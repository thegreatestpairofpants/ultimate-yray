Rice and Ketchup
by Yuri Andropov

A boy explains why he enjoys eating Rice and Ketchup together.

Lukas: I know some people might think it's weird, but I have a thing for rice and ketchup. It's just a simple combination, but to me, it's just perfect. The bland, fluffy rice paired with the sweet and tangy ketchup creates the perfect balance of flavors in my mouth.
So, yeah, that's why I like rice and ketchup. It's simple, it's comforting, and it just tastes so good. And I wouldn't have it any other way.




Drug Dealer
by Konstantin Chernenko

A guy explains why people shouldn't be concerned that he's selling drugs at school.

John: Ladies and Gentlemen, let me tell you about the most addicting business in town. Yes, you guessed it, I am the drug dealer of this school and I have been selling drugs to all of you for years now.

But I've noticed that lately, some of you have been worried about the effects of doing too much drugs. You're worried about the damage it might do to your brain, your waistline, and your overall health. I understand your concerns, but let me assure you, there's no need to worry.

First of all, a little bit of meth never hurt anyone. In fact, it's a great way to treat yourself and bring a smile to your face. The key is taking more. There more you do, the better you're gonna feel, a treat here and there won't hurt.

In conclusion, don't let your worries about drugs ruin the fun. Life is too short to say no to something that brings joy and happiness. Just remember to do as many drugs as you can, and you'll still be just fine. So, what are you waiting for? Come on down and treat yourself to a bunch of drugs today!




"Jonathan's Ex"
by Vyacheslav Molotov

A girl is fed up with being called "Jonathan's ex"

Belle: Can we talk for a minute? I've been meaning to address this for a while now and I just can't hold it in any longer. You see, every time I walk into a room, I hear the same thing, "Hey, there's Jonathan's ex." And I just can't take it anymore.

Do you know how that feels? To be reduced to just a label, a title, a footnote in someone else's story? I have a name, I have my own identity, my own story, and my own journey. But it seems like no one cares about that. All they care about is that I used to date someone.

I know it seems like a small thing, but it's not. It's a constant reminder that I'm not good enough on my own. That my worth is tied to someone else's name. And it's just not true. I am so much more than just a relationship status. I have passions, dreams, ambitions, and talents. I have a heart that beats with love and a soul that yearns for adventure.

So, I'm asking you, as a friend, to please stop calling me "Jonathan's ex." Call me by my name, ask me how I'm doing, show an interest in my life. Because I am more than just a label, I am a person. And I deserve to be seen and heard.

So PLEASE, just quit it.

So, to all those who think I'm egotistical, let me tell you, I am the best, and there's no one who can deny it. I am simply unmatched, unrivaled, and untouchable. And that's just the way it is.

Not that short
by Alexei Kosygin

A short kid claim he's not short, even though he's really short.

Louie: Listen, I know what you're thinking, you're thinking "Oh, he's just a short guy, he can't reach anything." But let me tell you, you couldn't be more wrong. I may only be 4 foot 8 inches, but that doesn't mean I'm short.

You see, it's all about perspective. Sure, compared to some people, I might come across as short, but compared to others, I'm tall. And, let's be real, there are plenty of people out there who are much shorter than me. So, if you look at it that way, I'm actually pretty tall.

And, it's not just my height, it's my attitude and confidence. I walk with my head held high and I own my height. I don't let anyone make me feel small or inferior. I am who I am, and I'm proud of it.

So, let's get one thing straight, I may be 4 foot 8 inches, but I am not short. I am just the right height for me. And that's all that matters.


Glee
by Lavrentiy Beria

Corinna is just super obsessed with Glee.

Corinna: You know what show I just can't get enough of? Glee! I mean, seriously, have you seen it? It's just so good! The music, the dancing, the drama, it's got it all.

Every time an episode comes on, I just get so absorbed in it. I find myself singing along with the characters, tapping my feet to the beat, and just feeling all the emotions right along with them. It's like I'm a part of the show or something.

And, I know some people might think I'm a little obsessed with Glee, but I just can't help it. It's just so addicting. I mean, I've watched every single episode multiple times, I've got the soundtrack on repeat, and I even dressed up as Rachel for Halloween.

But, it's not just about the music and the performance for me. It's about the message too. Glee is all about being yourself and following your dreams, no matter what anyone else might say. And, that's a message that I just can't get enough of. It gives me the inspiration and motivation I need to chase my own dreams, no matter how big or small they might be.

So, yeah, you can call me a Glee obsessed fan if you want, but I don't care. This show just speaks to me in a way that nothing else does. And, I'll be singing, dancing, and dreaming along with Glee for as long as it's on the air.





McCullough is just better
by Lev Kamenev

Madaline, after going to McCullough, finds it much better than her old school.

Madaline: Do you expect me to remember my time at Knox fondly? If so, I hate to break it to you, McCullough is just better. Like its not even close.

First of all, the people here are much better, we don't have any snobby, blonde, child actor favored by Ms. Jackson. Let alone kids dressed up as drug dealers on a daily basis. And sure, being a new kid was stressful at first, but once I got to know everyone, I realized something. McCullough is just better.

And the best part is the Theatre department. Everyone here is an amazing actor, who actually memorizes their lines, unlike someone over at Knox *cough* *cough* Maddox. And they're also some of the most amazing, most interesting and nicest people I've ever met.

So I hope you realize this now, McCullough is just the best. I have not a single regret on making this switch. I'll be having the time of my life here, and you'll be suffering over at Knox.





This class is just too much
by Grigory Zinoviev

Mrs. Johnson vents about her terrible 7th period class.

Mrs. Johnson: I can't take it anymore, this 7th period class is just too much. I've been teaching theatre for years, sharing my passion and my love for the art with countless students, but this class is just breaking me. They're disrespectful, they're inattentive, and they're making my job a living nightmare. I try to engage them, to get them to participate in class, but it's like talking to a brick wall. And, the behavior issues are just out of control. They're always testing the boundaries, arguing with me, and ignoring my instructions.

Sometimes, it all just becomes too much and I end up crying. I know that's not professional, but it's just how frustrated I am with this class. They're making me question everything I've ever known about teaching, and it's just so disheartening. These students don't seem to care about their grades, they don't seem to understand the effort and dedication it takes to perform on a stage.

I'm counting down the days until the end of the semester, but until then I've got to figure out a way to get through to these students and get them to appreciate the magic of theatre. I know it's not going to be easy, but I just have to try. Every day, I come in to that classroom, ready to fight the good fight, and every day I leave feeling defeated. But I won't give up, I won't let this class break me. I'll find a way to get through to them, even if it means crying every day.
